## Publisher's Foreword

At Jembon Publishing, we look for books that solve a problem most people don't even know they have.

When *The Pull Architecture* landed on our desk, we recognized it immediately: this was the missing manual for anyone who has ever wondered why being good at their job wasn't enough to get ahead. Why being generous didn't translate into being respected. Why showing up consistently still left them on the outside of rooms that mattered.

Dr. Nolan Vance doesn't offer platitudes about "being yourself" or "putting yourself out there." He offers architecture — a structured, layered system that transforms how you think about professional relationships from the ground up. Seven layers. Named tools. Concrete exercises. No fluff.

## Who This Book Is For

This book is for professionals at every stage who sense a gap between their competence and their influence. Specifically:

- **Early-career professionals** who want to build a reputation that compounds, not one that resets with every job change.
- **Mid-career executives** who've hit an invisible ceiling and suspect it's not about skill but about strategic positioning.
- **Entrepreneurs and founders** who need to build trust-based networks quickly and sustainably.
- **Anyone** who has been told they're "great to work with" but never gets the call when it counts.

## Why We Published This Book

The relationship advice market is crowded with books about charisma, small talk, and networking events. Most of them treat relationships as a performance. Dr. Vance treats them as engineering — and the results speak for themselves.

We believe *The Pull Architecture* will change how a generation of professionals thinks about social capital. Not as something you chase, but as something you build.

We're proud to bring this work to you.
